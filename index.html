About Us

WHO WE ARE: A close community of girls at Stuyvesant High School passionate about computer science. OUR MISSION: Provide a supportive environment to learn computer science and help close the gender cap in computer science.

OUR VALUES:

ü§ù Mentorship: We believe mentorship is crucial in a girl's journey through computer science. Our leadership board includes ten upperclassmen mentors who are here to support members in and out of meetings. Members are placed into small mentoring groups with these mentors to bond and receive whatever help they might need.

üí´ Skill-building: We want to expose members to a wide variety of topics in computer science and teach them about the endless paths one can take with computer science. Our weekly meetings, taught by student mentors, cover lessons on topics ranging from web development to data science to cybersecurity.

üì£ Presenting opportunities: Weekly lessons aren't the only thing club members experience! We are committed to organizing events with members of the STEM community, from guest speakers to (virtual) field trips.

Stuy X Columbia GWC | 12/4/20

Thank you to everyone who came to our first panel of the year! Today we heard from Marina Gemma, Dahlia Goshal and Mary McGreal, who talked about their experiences studying CS and applying CS to other scientific fields. The three of them demoed their own computer science projects, which included modeling atom activity, projecting images of the surface of Mercury, and a website modeling UX/UI design principles about how to dye your hair at home. Marina, Dahlia and Mary also answered many questions about how to get started in computer science and how to make the most of CS opportunities in college. Thank you Marina, Dahlia and Mary for being amazing panelists!
