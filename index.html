About Us

WHO WE ARE: A close community of girls at Stuyvesant High School passionate about computer science. OUR MISSION: Provide a supportive and fun environment to learn computer science and help close the gender gap in computer science.

OUR VALUES:

üí´ Skill-building: We want to expose members to a wide variety of beginner friendly topics in computer science and teach them about the endless paths one can take with computer science. Our weekly meetings, taught by student mentors, cover lessons on topics ranging from web development to data science to cybersecurity.

üì£ Presenting opportunities: Weekly lessons aren't the only thing club members experience! We are committed to organizing events with members of the STEM community, from guest speakers to field trips.

üèò Community: Girls Who Code @ Stuy isn't just a club‚Äîit's a community for girls to learn, bond and be inspired by each other. Stuy X Affectiva | 11/19/21

Thank you all for coming to our first panel of the year! Today, we heard from Lindsay Richter and Julia Semeraro, Software Engineers from Affectiva and former students from Northeastern's computer science program! Julia started us off by presenting a TedTalk about Affectiva and its purpose. Lindsay then explained how this technology can be used in promoting car safety. She showed us a video with examples of eye and expression tracking in automobiles. Next, we went over a slideshow about media analytics at Affectiva, which involves analyzing responses to media that you see. This media includes advertisements that businesses want Affectiva to analyze (ex: breakdowns of emotions and expressions while watching the ads). We also tried out the technology ourselves and saw our expressions get analyzed while watching an advertisement through a research demo. At the end, the panelists answered general questions about their experience in college and computer science.

Stuy X Columbia GWC | 12/4/20

Thank you to everyone who came to our first panel of the year! Today we heard from Marina Gemma, Dahlia Goshal and Mary McGreal, who talked about their experiences studying CS and applying CS to other scientific fields. Thank you Marina, Dahlia and Mary for being amazing panelists!
